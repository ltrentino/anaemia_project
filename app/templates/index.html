Why talk about anaemia?

Anaemia is a serious global public health problem that particularly affects young children and women. About 30% of women and 40% of children suffer from anaemia.

Anaemia is associated with increased mortality and morbidity worldwide. Negative outcomes include poor mental and physical development in children, and implications for productivity in adults. These outcomes have a negative flow on effect on the output of an economy.

WHO estimates that 42% of children less than 5 years of age and 40% of pregnant women worldwide are anaemic.
